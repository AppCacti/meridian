## The Simple Idea

Meridian Deal Partners is a private research desk for emerging family offices.

The buyer has capital, but not enough proprietary opportunity coverage. Meridian gives them a discreet external desk that maps, screens, and packages thesis-fit lower-middle-market opportunities before they build a full in-house team.

The public promise:

> A discreet deal desk before you build one in-house.

The private operating truth:

> We repoint the existing AppCacti/HegemonicGrowth research, AI, VA, outbound, and dashboard machine at a higher-value buyer.

Do not publicly sell this as HegemonicGrowth. The family-office market needs quiet finance language, not startup distribution language.

### Phase 2: Legal Guardrail

Goal: make sure the offer stays research-only and does not become regulated deal arranging.

Book one DIFC/ADGM-aware legal consult.

Give lawyer these docs:

- `docs/legal-review-questions.md`
- `docs/compliance-boundaries.md`
- `docs/research-retainer-outline.md`
- `docs/scope-of-services.md`

Ask them to approve:

- Flat-fee research-retainer structure.
- Website disclaimers.
- Outreach wording.

- Whether any introductions are allowed.
- What must never be said.

Until legal review is done, use this boundary:

Done when:

- You have approved wording for retainer, website, sample pack, and sales calls.

### Phase 5: Create The First Sample Pack

Goal: one beautiful proof asset.

Use:

- `docs/sample-pack-template.md`
- `docs/deal-teaser-template.md`
- `docs/research-qa-checklist.md`

The first pack should include:

- Cover.
- Mandate interpreted.
- 3 anonymized opportunities.
- Snapshot table for each.
- Fit rationale.
- Signals observed.
- Risks and unknowns.
- Next research questions.
- Scope disclaimer.

Important:

- Do not overclaim.
- Do not say "we can get you this deal."
- Do not reveal confidential information.
- Do not recommend investment.
- Make it feel like disciplined research, not a broker teaser blast.

Done when:

- A serious family-office principal would think: "This is useful. Send me more."

### Phase 6: Start Outreach

Goal: get replies, sample-pack requests, and discovery calls.

Use:

- `docs/outreach-sequence.md`

First week:

- 10-15 personalized touches per day.
- Only high-priority prospects.
- Do not mass blast before the message is proven.

Week two:

- Increase to 50-75 touches/week if reply quality is good.

Best opener:

> We mapped a few anonymized opportunities that look directionally relevant to your visible mandate. Worth sending the sample pack?

Why this works:

- It gives value before asking for time.
- It sounds discreet.
- It does not sound like generic agency outreach.
- It lets the prospect ask for the pack.

Track:

- Sent.
- Open/reply if available.
- Positive replies.
- Sample pack requested.
- Call booked.
- No response.
- Not fit.

Done when:

- 50 high-quality outreaches produce 4-6 replies.
- At least 2 prospects request the sample pack.
- At least 1-2 discovery calls are booked.

### Phase 7: Run Discovery Calls

Goal: diagnose mandate, pain, and buyer seriousness.

Use:

- `docs/discovery-call-script.md`
- `docs/client-onboarding-questionnaire.md`

Call structure:

1. Explain Meridian in 20 seconds.
2. Ask about mandate.
3. Ask where current deal access is weak.
4. Ask what makes an opportunity worth reviewing.
5. Clarify that Meridian is research-only.
6. Offer Founding Desk if pain is real.

Close line:

> The lightest way to test this is the Founding Desk. We run a 30-day mandate-led research cycle, deliver 3-5 thesis-fit opportunities, maintain the dashboard, and review the pack with you. It is a flat research retainer, not a transaction fee.

Done when:

- Prospect agrees to a paid pilot or asks for terms.

### Phase 8: Close First Cash

Goal: collect first retainer.

First offer:

- Founding Desk.

- 30 days.

What they get:

- Mandate capture.

Done when:

### Phase 10: Scale

Goal: make it pod-driven, not Tan-driven.

Unit of scale:

> One research pod serves 5-8 clients.

Pod roles:

- Researcher 1: prospect/company sourcing.
- Researcher 2: verification and source trail.
- Analyst/editor: memo production.
- QA lead: final quality check.
- Tan: sales, positioning, final review until delivery lead is ready.

Scale triggers:

- 2 paid clients: create repeatable dashboard and SOP.
- 3 paid clients: assign dedicated VA/research pod.
- 5 paid clients: hire or assign delivery lead.
- 8 paid clients: create second pod.

Markets:

1. UAE first.
2. Singapore after UAE proof.
3. London after UAE/Singapore proof.
4. US only after legal and process are stronger.

## What To Say In Plain English

Short version:

> Meridian is a private research desk for family offices. We help offices see more thesis-fit private-market opportunities before they build a full in-house origination team.

Long version:

> Newly formed offices often have capital before they have durable proprietary access. Meridian gives them a discreet external research apparatus: we map companies, screen for fit, package opportunity memos, and maintain a dashboard around the mandate. The structure is a flat research retainer, not a transaction fee.

## What Not To Say

Do not say:

- We broker deals.
- We get you allocations.
- We can get you into this company.
- We charge on capital deployed.
- We advise you what to buy.
- We guarantee proprietary deals.
